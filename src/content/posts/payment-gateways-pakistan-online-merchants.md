For Pakistan-based online merchants, a payment gateway is not just a checkout tool. It affects transaction success, integration effort, settlement flow, and how many customers actually complete payment.

This guide compares 5 popular payment gateways Pakistanis often ask about:

1. XPay  
2. PayFast  
3. HBLPay Checkout  
4. Bank Alfalah IPG  
5. PayPro  

## Quick answer

If you want the safest short list for a formal online business in Pakistan, start with **PayFast**, **HBLPay Checkout**, and **Bank Alfalah IPG**. **XPay** is interesting if onsite checkout and routing flexibility matter to you, while **PayPro** is attractive if you want a wider collections stack that also covers invoicing and over-the-counter flows. Keep reading if you are deciding between bank-backed acquiring, local PSP scale, and a more flexible payments layer.

## 1) XPay

XPay focuses heavily on onsite checkout, dynamic routing, developer friendliness, and merchant flexibility. Its official pages pitch it as a way to reduce payment friction and improve transaction success rather than just as a basic redirect gateway.

XPay is best for merchants who care about checkout conversion, want a cleaner onsite payment experience, and are comfortable working with a more modern payments stack.

The commercial setup can depend on the merchant IDs and acquiring relationships behind your implementation. Verify settlement terms, supported instruments, and exactly which gateways or banking partners are live for your business category.

## 2) PayFast

PayFast presents itself as a licensed local payments platform with cards, bank accounts, wallets, and QR-based rails. Its official public material also highlights a first-mover position around Raast P2M merchant payments, which matters for local checkout relevance.

PayFast is a strong fit for merchants who want a local PSP-style platform with broad payment-method coverage and strong Pakistan e-commerce familiarity.

Do not assume your category will get the same commercial terms as a large enterprise merchant. Check integration support, onboarding requirements, settlements, refunds, and actual success rates for your business model.

## 3) HBLPay Checkout

HBLPay Checkout is positioned as a single integration for card schemes, account-based payments, and wallet-based flows. The official HBL page also highlights multicurrency support, recurring transactions, and plugins for faster merchant onboarding.

HBLPay Checkout is best for merchants who want a bank-backed payment stack with broader formal infrastructure and support for more than one payment mode.

Bank-backed solutions can bring more structure, but they can also mean more formal onboarding and operational checks. Confirm the exact instruments, currencies, plugins, and merchant category fit available to your business today.

## 4) Bank Alfalah IPG

Bank Alfalah’s retail payment solutions page combines POS and internet payment gateway capabilities under one merchant offering. The IPG section emphasizes MPGS power, card-scheme coverage, PKR and USD support, tokenization, recurring payments, and a real-time merchant portal.

Bank Alfalah IPG is best for established merchants who want a recognized acquirer relationship and a card-heavy gateway with broader enterprise-style features.

This kind of setup is often better suited to more formal businesses than to very small experimental stores. Check onboarding documentation, rollout timelines, and whether the bank’s merchant process fits your speed requirements.

## 5) PayPro

PayPro goes beyond checkout and positions itself as a one-window collections platform for digital invoicing, payment gateway integration, OTC collections, and recurring billing. Its gateway pages also emphasize cards, wallets, online banking partners, and local merchant integrations.

PayPro is best for businesses that want a broader collections workflow, not just a card checkout button on a website.

PayPro states that charges vary by business, and its refund timelines can be materially longer than what some merchants expect. Make sure the commercial model and operational processes fit your customer-support burden.

## How to choose your option in 10 minutes

### Step 1: Match product type to your behavior

- Decide whether you need only checkout, or a broader payment-and-collections workflow.
- Remove options that do not support the payment instruments your customers actually use.

### Step 2: Stress test your usage assumptions

- Check merchant onboarding, plugin support, API quality, and refund handling before you sign.
- Ask about settlement timing and failed-payment handling, not just headline transaction success.

### Step 3: Set controls before committing

- Run a short commercial comparison based on your expected transaction mix.
- Keep one backup plan in case a gateway category review or technical issue slows your launch.

## FIRE Rule for Payment Gateway Decisions

The best payment gateway is the one that increases successful collections without adding operational drag. A slightly better sales pitch means very little if the gateway creates integration headaches, settlement friction, or poor refund handling.
