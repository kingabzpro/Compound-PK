Pakistan-based investors looking beyond PSX usually end up comparing a small group of international brokers and introducing brokers. The better fit depends on whether you value direct global access, lower commissions, easier support, or a broker setup that feels less intimidating at the start.

This guide compares 5 popular global brokers Pakistanis often ask about:

1. Interactive Brokers  
2. CapTrader  
3. Zacks Trade  
4. TradeStation Global  
5. MEXEM  

## Quick answer

If you want the clearest direct route into global markets, **Interactive Brokers** is still the benchmark in this list. **CapTrader**, **Zacks Trade**, **TradeStation Global**, and **MEXEM** are more relevant when you want a different fee structure, a different support layer, or a more tailored wrapper around broader market access. Keep reading if you are deciding between direct IBKR-style access and broker arrangements that feel more guided.

## 1) Interactive Brokers

Interactive Brokers is the most direct global-access story here. Its official pages highlight access to stocks, options, futures, currencies, bonds, and funds across 170+ markets from one platform, which is exactly why it remains the reference point for serious self-directed global investors.

Interactive Brokers is best for investors who want broad market access, direct control, and a broker that can scale with a more advanced portfolio over time.

The breadth comes with complexity. New investors often underestimate the learning curve around platform setup, funding, tax forms, permissions, and product selection.

## 2) CapTrader

CapTrader positions itself as a global broker with access to more than 170 markets, 29 currencies, and a very wide product menu across stocks, ETFs, options, futures, forex, and more. Its public material also leans more into education and options trading support than many simple retail broker pitches.

CapTrader is best for investors and active traders who want broad global access but also value a more guided broker experience and stronger options-related emphasis.

Do not choose it only because the product menu is huge. You still need to compare the real fee schedule, platform workflow, tax-document handling, and whether the support layer matters enough for your use case.

## 3) Zacks Trade

Zacks Trade is very clearly aimed at active traders. Its official site emphasizes low-cost stock and ETF trades, low option-contract pricing, lower margin rates, and research tools tied to the Zacks ecosystem.

Zacks Trade is best for active self-directed traders who want market access plus a recognizable research-and-support angle.

This is not the cleanest beginner-first setup for someone who only wants to buy a few ETFs and ignore markets for years. Margin, options, and an active-trading tilt can pull the wrong user into unnecessary complexity.

## 4) TradeStation Global

TradeStation Global is generally positioned as an international route into a TradeStation-style trading experience for non-US clients. The product is usually relevant to users who want international brokerage access with a more trading-oriented platform identity than a plain basic broker dashboard.

TradeStation Global is best for internationally minded traders who care about platform experience and want to explore a trading-oriented setup rather than a bare-bones investing flow.

This is the name in the list that most needs a verify-first approach. Confirm current Pakistan onboarding eligibility, entity structure, pricing, and support path directly from the latest official material before treating it as a live option.

## 5) MEXEM

MEXEM positions itself as a full-package broker with access to global stocks, ETFs, options, futures, bonds, and more from a single account. Its public materials also emphasize local support and a broad international product range rather than a narrow single-asset pitch.

MEXEM is best for users who want broad global access but still value the idea of a more service-led broker relationship instead of using the largest direct broker brand by default.

Product breadth can look attractive, but it can also create unnecessary complexity for basic long-term investors. Compare the real commission model, support quality, and funding practicality before you assume it beats the simpler direct route.

## How to choose your option in 10 minutes

### Step 1: Match product type to your behavior

- Decide whether you want direct global brokerage access or a more guided broker relationship.
- Remove options that feel built for active trading if your real plan is passive long-term investing.

### Step 2: Stress test your usage assumptions

- Check onboarding from Pakistan, funding routes, permissions, and base-currency handling.
- Review the exact commissions on the products you will actually trade, not the ones you admire in theory.

### Step 3: Set controls before committing

- Keep your product list honest: stocks and ETFs only, or options too.
- If you are new, start with the platform that makes disciplined investing easier, not more exciting.

## FIRE Rule for Global Broker Decisions

The best broker is the one that gives you the market access you need without pushing you into unnecessary complexity. More instruments and more trading tools are not automatically better if your real goal is simply to buy and hold a sound portfolio.
